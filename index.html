FAQ

ENUM & Enums Swap
— Answered

Everything you need to know about ENUM, TRON DeFi, swapping, staking, borrowing and using Enums Swap.

What is Enums Swap?+
Enums Swap is a non-custodial DeFi platform built on TRON. It combines best-rate token swapping, ENUM staking for yield, on-chain borrowing, liquidity pool provision, and credit — all in one interface, with no KYC or account registration required.
What is ENUM token?+
ENUM is the native TRC-20 utility and governance token of the Enums Swap ecosystem on TRON. It is used to pay reduced protocol fees, participate in staking rewards, serve as collateral for borrowing and credit, and take part in governance votes on protocol parameters.
How do I swap tokens on Enums Swap?+
Connect your TronLink wallet by clicking "Connect Wallet" in the top right. Select your input and output token in the Swap widget. Enter your desired amount, review the quoted rate, price impact, and route. Click "Swap Now" and approve the transaction in your wallet. TRON finality is under 3 seconds, so your swapped tokens arrive almost instantly.
How do I stake ENUM on Enums Swap?+
Navigate to the Staking tab in the Enums Swap widget or click "Stake ENUM" in the header. Connect your wallet, enter the amount you want to stake, and select a lock period — flexible, 30 days, 90 days, or 365 days. Longer locks earn higher APY bonuses. Confirm the transaction in TronLink and your staking position activates immediately, with rewards accruing in real time.
What APY can I earn staking ENUM?+
ENUM staking APY is dynamic and depends on the total amount staked in the protocol, your chosen lock period, and current market conditions. Flexible staking earns the base APY, while longer lock periods earn bonus multipliers. Exact APY figures are displayed live in the Staking panel after connecting your wallet, and rewards are distributed in ENUM.
How does borrowing work on Enums Swap?+
Open the Borrow tab, deposit ENUM, TRX, or another supported asset as collateral, and choose an asset to borrow such as USDT. The interface shows your maximum loan-to-value ratio, borrow APR, and liquidation price before you confirm. Once approved, the borrowed asset is sent directly to your wallet, and you can repay at any time to release your collateral.
What is the difference between Borrow and Credit on Enums Swap?+
Borrow lets you take out a single fixed loan against deposited collateral, typically used for a specific borrowing need. Credit opens a revolving line backed by your ENUM holdings, which you can draw down and repay repeatedly without opening a new position each time. Both use the same collateral health-factor monitoring to protect against liquidation.
Can I provide liquidity with ENUM on Enums Swap?+
Yes. The Pool tab lets you add liquidity to ENUM trading pairs including ENUM/USDT, ENUM/TRX, and ENUM/ETH. As a liquidity provider, you earn a proportional share of all trading fees generated by that pool, in addition to any liquidity mining incentives. You receive LP tokens representing your position, which can be staked for additional ENUM rewards in supported incentive programs.
What wallets are supported by Enums Swap?+
Enums Swap supports TronLink (recommended for swaps on TRON), MetaMask, Trust Wallet, WalletConnect (200+ compatible wallets), Coinbase Wallet, Ledger hardware wallet, Trezor, and Phantom. For swaps on TRON, TronLink is the most seamless option. For cross-chain operations involving ETH or BNB Chain, any EVM-compatible wallet works alongside the Enums Swap interface.
What fees does Enums Swap charge?+
Enums Swap charges a protocol fee starting at 0.01% of swap value — among the lowest in the TRON DeFi ecosystem. You also pay the underlying TRON network gas fee, which is typically under $0.01 per transaction. The full price displayed in your quote is all-inclusive, so there are no hidden fees. What you see is exactly what you receive.
Is Enums Swap safe? Is it audited?+
Enums Swap is fully non-custodial — your tokens remain in your wallet until the moment a swap executes atomically on-chain. Smart contracts are audited by leading security firms. MEV protection shields every swap from front-running bots. If a cross-chain operation cannot complete for any reason, your funds are automatically returned to your originating wallet. No private keys are ever required by Enums Swap.
How are staking rewards calculated?+
ENUM staking on Enums Swap means depositing your tokens into a staking smart contract in exchange for yield rewards. Rewards are calculated based on: (1) your share of the total ENUM staked pool, (2) the protocol's reward emission rate, and (3) any lock-period bonus multiplier you've activated. Rewards are distributed continuously and can be claimed or compounded at any time through the Enums Swap interface.
Can I unstake ENUM early?+
Flexible staking (no lock period) allows you to unstake ENUM at any time with no penalty. For locked positions (30d, 90d, 365d), early withdrawal may incur a penalty fee, which is redirected to remaining stakers as a bonus. The exact early unstake penalty percentage is shown in your staking dashboard. After the lock period expires, you can withdraw your ENUM and accrued rewards freely.
What is the total supply of ENUM?+
ENUM has a fixed total supply of 1,000,000,000 (1 billion) TRC-20 tokens. A portion is allocated to liquidity incentives, staking rewards, the protocol treasury, and the founding team under a multi-year vesting schedule. The exact circulating supply and current emission schedule are published in the Enums Swap documentation and updated as new tranches unlock.
How fast are swaps on TRON?+
Swaps on the TRON network finalize in under 3 seconds thanks to TRON's delegated proof-of-stake (DPoS) consensus mechanism. This makes Enums Swap one of the fastest DEX experiences available — far faster than Ethereum-based swaps which take 12–60 seconds or more. For cross-chain bridge operations, finality depends on the destination chain and typically completes within 1–5 minutes.
What is slippage and how should I set it?+
Slippage is the maximum acceptable difference between the quoted price and the actual executed price, caused by market movement between the moment you request a quote and when the transaction confirms on-chain. For most stable pairs on TRON, 0.1%–0.5% is sufficient. For large trades or volatile tokens, use 1%. Enums Swap's "Auto" mode dynamically sets the optimal slippage for each pair based on historical volatility.
Does Enums Swap require KYC or account registration?+
No. Enums Swap is completely permissionless — you never need to create an account, provide an email address, or complete KYC identity verification of any kind. Simply connect a compatible wallet (TronLink on TRON, or MetaMask for cross-chain swaps) and you're ready to trade instantly. Your wallet address is your identity on Enums Swap.
How does MEV protection work?+
MEV (Maximal Extractable Value) bots monitor public mempools and front-run swap transactions, buying a token before your trade and selling it to you at an inflated price. Enums Swap routes all swap transactions through private relay mempools where bots cannot detect or front-run them. This protection saves users on average $10–$70 on large trades and ensures you always receive the price quoted — not the price after a bot has skimmed your transaction.
How does Enums Swap find the best swap rate?+
The routing engine simultaneously queries 300+ DEX pools on TRON — including SunSwap, JustSwap, and dozens of smaller AMMs — and uses a pathfinding algorithm to split your order optimally across multiple pools. This minimizes price impact and maximizes the output token amount. Quotes refresh every 5 seconds to ensure you always act on accurate, real-time pricing.
What are the risks of providing liquidity?+
The primary risk of liquidity provision is impermanent loss (IL) — a temporary loss that occurs when the price ratio between the two pooled tokens changes significantly from when you deposited. If one asset's price rises sharply, you earn less appreciation than simply holding. However, trading fee income can offset or exceed IL over time. Enums Swap displays an IL estimator in the Pool dashboard to help you evaluate this tradeoff.
What is the ENUM/USDT liquidity pool?+
The ENUM/USDT pool is the highest-volume trading pair on Enums Swap, facilitating most ENUM-to-stablecoin conversions and vice versa. Liquidity providers deposit equal USD-value amounts of ENUM and USDT (TRC-20) into the pool and receive ENUM/USDT LP tokens in return. These LP tokens represent your share of the pool and earn trading fees automatically, and can also be staked in Enums Swap incentive vaults for additional ENUM rewards.
Can I get a preview swap quote without connecting a wallet?+
You can enter amounts and preview route breakdowns in the Enums Swap widget without connecting a wallet. Rate quotes and price impact estimates are visible immediately. Your wallet balance is not shown until you connect, and you cannot execute a swap without a wallet connection — but you can research and plan your trade fully before committing to a wallet connection.
What is price impact in a swap?+
Price impact is the percentage difference between the mid-market price and the actual price you receive on your swap, caused by your trade moving the pool's price curve. Small swaps relative to pool size have negligible price impact. Large trades in shallow pools can have significant price impact (1–5%+). Enums Swap's smart routing splits large orders across multiple pools to minimize price impact, displaying the estimated impact clearly before you confirm.
Is Enums Swap available on mobile?+
Yes. Enums Swap is a fully responsive web application accessible on any device — desktop, tablet, or smartphone. For mobile swaps on TRON, use the TronLink mobile app's built-in dApp browser to access enums-swap.vercel.app directly. For EVM chains on mobile, MetaMask Mobile or Trust Wallet's dApp browser work seamlessly with the Enums Swap interface.
How do I claim my ENUM staking rewards?+
Staking rewards accrue continuously in your staking dashboard. To claim them, navigate to the Staking tab in Enums Swap, connect your wallet, and click "Claim Rewards." Confirm the transaction in TronLink and your earned ENUM transfers to your wallet immediately. You can also choose to auto-compound your rewards — re-staking them instantly to maximize your yield position without a separate transaction.
What happens if my borrow position is liquidated?+
If your collateral value drops below the liquidation threshold defined by your position's health factor, the protocol automatically sells a portion of your collateral to repay the outstanding loan plus a liquidation fee. You can avoid liquidation by adding more collateral or repaying part of your loan whenever your health factor approaches the warning threshold shown in the Borrow and Credit dashboards.
How does Enums Swap handle failed transactions?+
If a swap transaction fails — due to slippage exceeding your tolerance, insufficient gas, or a network issue — your tokens remain safely in your wallet. Nothing is lost. The transaction simply doesn't execute, and Enums Swap displays an error message explaining the cause. For cross-chain operations that fail mid-way, funds are automatically returned to the source chain wallet via the protocol's rollback mechanism, typically within minutes.
Can I swap to stablecoins directly?+
Yes. Enums Swap supports direct token-to-stablecoin swaps including ENUM → USDT (TRC-20), ENUM → USDC, and ENUM → TUSD. These are the most liquid pairs on TRON and typically have the lowest slippage and best price impact. The ENUM/USDT pair on TRON is the dominant liquidity venue, with millions of dollars in daily trading volume routed through Enums Swap.
How do I get TRX to pay gas fees for swaps?+
TRON transactions require a small amount of TRX for network energy and bandwidth fees, which are extremely cheap — typically under $0.01 per swap. You can acquire TRX from any major centralized exchange (Binance, OKX, Kraken) and withdraw it directly to your TronLink wallet address. Once you have a small TRX balance (5–20 TRX is enough for many transactions), you're ready to use Enums Swap without interruption.
Does Enums Swap support cross-chain bridging?+
Enums Swap integrates with established cross-chain bridge infrastructure, allowing ENUM and other TRC-20 tokens to move between TRON and 30+ networks including Ethereum, BNB Chain, Polygon, and Arbitrum. Bridging is accessible from the chain selector in the Swap widget — choose a non-TRON destination and the interface automatically routes your transaction through the appropriate bridge protocol.
